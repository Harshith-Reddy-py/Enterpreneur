Builder's Mindset

Truths that matter more than motivation

Failure is Required

Every significant innovation involves failure. SpaceX failed 3 times before reaching orbit. The question isn't whether you'll fail, but what you'll learn from it.

IIT is a Tool, Not a Goal

IIT provides network, resources, and credibility. But it doesn't guarantee success. Use it properly: build projects, find co-founders, access research.

Builders Win

In the long run, those who can actually build things (products, companies, systems) have more leverage than those who only analyze or manage.

Think in Decades

Musk started SpaceX in 2002. Tesla in 2004. Neuralink in 2016. These are 10-20 year bets. Most people overestimate what they can do in 1 year and underestimate what they can do in 10.

First Principles Thinking

Don't optimize existing solutions. Question fundamental assumptions. What's physically possible? What's the fundamental constraint? Build from there.

Solve World-Scale Problems

Energy. Space. AI. Transport. Biology. These domains affect billions and have decades of growth ahead. Find what fascinates you enough to work on for 20 years.
